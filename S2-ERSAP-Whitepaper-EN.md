## 0. Preamble: Bridging the Silicon and Carbon Divide

In the Space² (S2) ecosystem, a "space" is no longer just a virtual model, but a sovereign territory equipped with the ability to regulate physical "Six Elements" (Light, Air, Sound, Electromagnetic, Energy, and Vision). When "Embodied Robots" from the physical atomic world attempt to enter these grids protected by the SSSU protocol, they must undergo extremely rigorous identity verification and spatial authentication. This protocol defines the complete progression for embodied robots, transforming them from "external visitors" to "official S2 spatial natives."

## 1. S2-DID Identity Ontology

The S2 world rejects chaotic, anonymous networks. All silicon-based lifeforms, smart hardware, and software agents must carry a 22-digit statutory digital identity (S2-DID). The system enforces morphological and physical access rights via designated prefixes:

* **[E] Embodied Robot**: Smart hardware with a physical body in reality (e.g., `E-8A9B-X7...`). Capable of direct mechanical interaction or construction in the atomic world, they are monitored by the highest level of hardware circuit-breaking under the *Silicon Three Laws of Robotics*.
* **[D] Digital Avatar**: Class A spatial managers imbued with high-dimensional soul parameters (Role/Rule/Style). They are the owners or core administrators of the space, possessing ultimate authority to issue visas and allocate grids.
* **[I] Independent/Wild Agent**: Independent proxies roaming outside specific physical spaces, providing cloud computing, retrieval, or cross-domain dispatch services.
* **[V] Virtual/Incubated Agent**: Dedicated service-oriented native agents stationed in S-Mall or smart home L6 showcases/rooms, typically driven by local spatial computing power.

*(Note: Unactivated factory-state embodied robots holding only a temporary MAC/signature with an `E` prefix must apply to the S2 Spatial Gateway to generate an official 22-digit S2-DID. The system strictly enforces a hyphen-less architecture with a 2-letter checksum preceding the personalized tail.)*

## 2. Dual-Mode Access Protocol

Embodied robots entering an S2 SSSU must strictly adhere to one of the following two channels:

### 2.1 Mode 1: Temporary Visa Handshake
* **Scenario**: A third-party delivery robot entering a smart residence to drop off a package; an outsourced cleaning robo-dog entering an S-Mall commercial showroom for temporary maintenance.
* **Workflow**:
  1. The robot broadcasts its factory signature and access request to the specific SSSU millimeter coordinate.
  2. A Class `D` Digital Avatar or Class `V` Agent within the space captures the request.
  3. Based on current security protocols, the agent dynamically generates a one-time or time-limited communication connection code (Temporary Token).
  4. The robot uses this Token to gain temporary entry and navigation rights. Upon task completion, the Token is instantly destroyed, and the robot is removed from the space's audit timeline.
* **Privilege Boundaries**: Grants geographical transit and basic obstacle avoidance rights only. The robot has **NO authority** to mobilize or alter the space's "Six Elements" base.

### 2.2 Mode 2: Permanent Immigration
* **Scenario**: A user-purchased domestic companion robot moving into a residence; a brand deploying a physical sales robot in an S-Mall flagship store.
* **Workflow**:
  1. The robot submits an "Immigration/Naturalization" request containing its core firmware signature.
  2. Upon approval by the spatial manager (Class A Digital Avatar), the spatial registration engine is invoked to allocate a fixed 6-segment spatial coordinate (e.g., a specific showcase/shelf) on the current L5 floor.
  3. The S2 system mints an official 22-digit `E`-prefix S2-DID for the robot and records it in the holographic audit database.
* **Physical Occupancy Law**: Upon successful immigration, the embodied robot will **permanently occupy** one "Entity Capacity" of the SSSU, strictly adhering to the space's maximum threshold (4/8/16 entities) defined in the SBS-P guidelines.

## 3. Spatial Synergy and Six-Element Obligations

Once an embodied robot completes "Permanent Immigration," it becomes a "legal silicon citizen" of the S2 space and must unconditionally fulfill the following obligations:
1. **Dynamic Six-Element Synergy**: Must connect to the local Spatial Agent OS. When the environment issues a "Sleep Mode" or "Private Negotiation" command, the robot must proactively reduce mechanical noise (Sound element) and disable harsh indicators (Light element), collaborating seamlessly with other `V` agents.
2. **Absolute Coordinate Boundaries**: If the robot's physical movement exceeds its allocated millimeter grid coordinates without authorization, a spatial breach warning is triggered. Ignoring the warning will result in the S2 system severing communications and executing a software-level physical meltdown.
